The first Jump instruction is conditional because it needs to check if the certain conditions are met in order to be executed by the program.

Flags determine whether the jump will be executed or not as well as whether the jump is conditional or unconditional.

2.2: What is the purpose of the Jump instruction after the consequent?

The purpose of the Jump instruction after the consequent is to skip the alternative.

2.3: Why is there no need for a corresponding Jump instruction after the alternative?

There is no need for a corresponding jump instruction after the alternative because there are no more conditions to evaluate for the program.

There is also no further execution that has to be done.
